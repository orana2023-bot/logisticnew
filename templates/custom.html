Essential Shipping Documents

Commercial Invoice

The primary document used by customs for control, valuation, and duty determination.

Packing List

Detailed information about the contents, weight, and dimensions of each package.

Certificate of Origin

Declaration of where the goods were manufactured to determine eligibility for trade deals.

Bill of Lading

The contract between the owner of the goods and the carrier, acting as a title of goods.

Frequently Asked Questions

Incoterms are standardized trade terms that define the responsibilities of buyers and sellers for the delivery of goods under sales contracts.

This depends on the agreed Incoterms. Generally, in DDP (Delivered Duty Paid), the seller pays, while in DAP (Delivered at Place), the buyer is responsible.

Delays often occur due to missing documentation, incorrect value declarations, or physical inspections required by local authorities.

Product classification involves assigning a Harmonized System (HS) code to your goods. This code is crucial for determining applicable duties, taxes, and regulations. Accurate classification prevents delays and ensures compliance with international trade laws. Our experts can assist in correctly classifying your products.

Customs duties are taxes levied on imported goods, calculated based on the product's value, origin, and classification. Other taxes like VAT or GST may also apply. Ensuring full compliance with all trade agreements and local regulations is vital to avoid penalties, fines, and shipment delays. We provide guidance on navigating these complexities.

Certain goods are restricted (requiring special permits, licenses, or certifications) or entirely prohibited from import/export due to safety, environmental, health, or national security concerns. Examples include hazardous materials, firearms, certain agricultural products, and cultural artifacts. It is crucial to verify all restrictions for your specific goods and destination country before shipping.

Both importers and exporters bear significant responsibilities, including providing accurate and complete documentation, correctly classifying products, and adhering to all customs regulations. Maintaining thorough records is essential. Engaging with an experienced customs broker can greatly simplify the process, mitigate risks, and ensure efficient and timely customs clearance for your shipments.
